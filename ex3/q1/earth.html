This is the Earth

Earth is a remarkable planet, known for its dynamic surface and ability to sustain life. It is part of the Solar System and possesses unique features that make it suitable for habitation. Earth's geological and atmospheric properties set it apart from other celestial bodies.

  • Earth's circumference is approximately 40,075 kilometers (24,901 miles) at the equator.
  • It is the densest planet in the Solar System, with a core composed mainly of iron and nickel.
  • Earth's crust is divided into tectonic plates that move and interact, causing earthquakes and forming mountains.
  • The planet experiences seasons due to its axial tilt of about 23.5 degrees.
  • Earth's hydrosphere includes freshwater sources like rivers and lakes, in addition to its vast oceans.
  • The atmosphere contains layers, such as the troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, and thermosphere, each serving distinct purposes.
  • Earth's biosphere supports over 8.7 million species, including plants, animals, and microorganisms.
  • Polar regions contain large ice caps, which influence global climate and sea levels.
